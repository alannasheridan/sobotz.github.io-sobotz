AI Facial Recognition
(Reached 20+ People | Since 2023)
Project Invent is a competition dedicated to using technology to improve the lives of others. To solve authentication issues at places that needed to track attendance, our Software Subteam created affordable Facial Recognition Attendance Software for the Police Athletic League. Our group was awarded $500 and was given permission to further develop the software to share with other organizations.

Bear Fundraiser
(Reached 200+ People | Since 2023)
Before and during Valentine’s Day week, our team has a table during lunch periods where members volunteer to sell teddy bears. Many students pass by our table and stop by to buy a bear or talk to our members.









Board of Education Meeting
(Reached 200+ People | Since 2018)
Annually, we present at our district’s Board of Education meeting to show our appreciation for the team’s biggest sponsor, South Orangetown Central School District. We drive our robot and demonstrate its functions, have members share their experiences on SO BOTZ, and give our Impact award presentation to show how much our team has accomplished in the past year.

Crescendo Cafe
(Reached 100+ People | Since 2023)
Starting in 2023, we began the Crescendo Cafe, an afterschool cafe that sells a variety of food and snacks for students at clubs. With this, we’ve fundraised over $400+ and gained many new members by promoting and talking about our team to students passing by.

CTF Game
(Reached 5000+ People | Since 2024)
This year, our team decided to help fundraise for tumor research during our school’s annual Children’s Tumor Foundation (CTF) Basketball Game. During the half-time of all four games, we drove our robot around the gym and collected donations that went to the CTF.

Donating 1K
(In 2020)
2020 was a tough year for many families with COVID causing a national quarantine. SO BOTZ saw how much our community was struggling with this new, unfamiliar challenge, so we donated $1,000.00 to several families through South Orangetown Family Engagement Center.









Friends Of The Troops
(Reached 150+ People | Since 2024)
Recently, one of our alumni, who served at the Air Force Academy for two years, came back to become a mentor for our Software subteam. His service in the military inspired us to connect with Friends Of The Troops, a volunteer-led nonprofit organization that supports and honors Veterans and members of the U.S. Armed Forces. To reach out to these veterans, our members made cards to send to several Veteran Homes on Valentine’s Day, and we make monthly Birthday and appreciation cards.

Holiday Alumni Party
(Reached 15+ People | Since 2023)
Recently, we began to host a Holiday Alumni Party where we invite all of our alumni to join our team in enjoying food, activities, and connecting with our members. This past year, we had fifteen alumni come back, introduce themselves to our current members, and talk about their experience on SO BOTZ. After the party, we gained another mentor as one of our alumni wanted to help our team’s effort once again.

Karamu
(Reached 50+ Schools | Since 2023)
Our team attended a Kiwimbi auction known as Karamu, where we shared the progress of SO BOTZ and were honored for our efforts with the STAGSS robotics team. The success of STAGSS motivated numerous people to donate, raising a total of $61,643, which went towards the general education of 50+ schools in Kenya to help them access better education.

Lowes Fundraiser
(Reached 100+ People | Since 2023)
To fundraise for both the STAGSS robotics team and our own, we hosted a stand in front of our local Lowes. To encourage people walking by to donate to STAGSS or help our team by buying materials or tools, we talked about our team’s progress over the years and the founding of STAGSS.








Penguin Plunge Mario Kart
(Reached 27+ People | Since 2024)
The Penguin Plunge is a not-for-profit organization that helps families of seriously ill children meet their medical costs. Every year, this organization raises money for four children by having people volunteer and sponsor those who plunge into the Hudson River. As this year’s plunge is on the same day as our Week 0 event, we created a Mario Kart Tournament for our high schoolers as another way to fundraise and spread awareness for these children.

Soup Kitchen
(Reached 100+ People | Since 2024)
Every month for three hours, our members volunteer at the Soup Kitchen in the First Nyack Church to serve healthy, home-cooked meals, in a restaurant-style setting, and provide groceries and take-home meals to anyone who needs it. While acting as their waiters, we answer questions about who we are and what we like to do and listen to any stories they want to share.

Sponsor Tours
(Reached 16+ People | Since 2019)
We have always sought to maintain a good relationship with our sponsors by giving them regular updates through emails and our social media posts. To help promote these connections, we started to give our sponsors tours of our workspace while talking to them about our organization, competitions, and member experiences.

Werfen Project
(Reached 20+ People | Since 2023)
Over the past year, our design team has been working with one of our sponsors, Werfen, to design a promotional mascot based on one of their blood sampling machines.
